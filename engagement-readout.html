🎯 Strategic Takeaways 7 cross-cutting themes

P1 NEW 1. Tokenisation and tokenised deposits are live — compliance infrastructure is the bottleneck, and it's ours to fill

Tokenised bank deposits emerged as a distinct priority that deserves focus alongside stablecoins and securities tokenisation. The BoE Digital Securities Sandbox has 16 exchanges preparing to live-issue securities. StanChart — the only GSIB live across all asset types — described bolting a fiat leg onto digital transactions as "not scalable." StanChart and HSBC are both launching stablecoins in Hong Kong by year-end. SWIFT is running an MVP on tokenised deposits by end of June (messaging only, no settlement). MAS is issuing a wholesale CBDC bill for tokenised deposit settlement. UBS's Qivalis project is building interoperable stablecoins alongside tokenised bonds and gold.

"New rails don't remove the need for market integrity. Investor protection will rely as much on the credential that travels with it as with the asset." — John O'Neill, HSBC

The critical insight: stablecoins and tokenised deposits are not substitutes — they are different use cases. Stablecoins are open-loop and permissionless; tokenised deposits work within banks or clearing networks. The compliance gap is in both. Banking Circle's proof-of-reserve attestation travelling with transactions (BIS/MAS MVP) points directly at the tooling we should build. Paxos: "GENIUS-compliant stables will drive tokenisation" — stablecoin regulation will pull tokenised deposits into mainstream. GFTN has invited Chainalysis to help build a tokenisation blueprint per jurisdiction and to join the SFF lineup. GSIBs are building now. Compliance tooling is what they're missing. This is not a future topic.

Sources: DMC Taskforce PZF Tokenised FM Roundtable PZF Main Stage (Simon Taylor, UBS CEO) Stripe Closed Door (Paxos, Banking Circle) Matthias bilaterals (SWIFT, GFTN, MAS)
→ Inform: Product (critical) — credential-with-asset tooling + tokenised deposit compliance are immediate market needs. Sales — StanChart + HSBC HK near-term; DMC members are clients. Strategy — elevate tokenised deposits alongside stablecoins. BD — GFTN SFF invitation, SWIFT scaling.

P1 2. Stablecoin frameworks are converging — but multi-issuance and cross-border friction remain unsolved

Three jurisdictions advancing simultaneously: UK (BoE systemic stablecoins), EU (MiCA / MiCA 2), US (GENIUS Act). The BoE's most innovative feature is a liquidity facility allowing issuers to liquidate 70% of sovereign debt reserves if private markets fail, with a walked-back holding limit and a ~£40bn issuance cap. Patrick Hansen (Circle) identified three cross-border friction dimensions: classification, treatment as money, and foreign stablecoin recognition. Multi-issuance is the key bottleneck — Ripple is blocked by Luxembourg regulators citing legal uncertainty; Coinbase flagged Brazil and Canada debating the same issue. Paxos observed redemption rights matter in theory but "nobody uses them in practice" and compliance with many jurisdictions is "so expensive we can't do this" — pointing directly at Tether. Paradigm's Justin Slaughter gave CLARITY <20% odds in July.

Sources: PZF Stablecoin Frameworks RT Stripe Closed Door PZF CEO Dialogue RUSI Stablecoins Workshop Matthias bilaterals (BoE, Circle)
→ Inform: Product — multi-issuance tracking, reserve monitoring. Legal — MiCA 2 reserve changes. EMEA Sales — Ripple geolocation opportunity. Research — BoE liquidity facility, CLARITY <20%.

P1 3. Privacy infrastructure is outpacing supervisory capability — architecture choices now determine compliance outcomes

Two contrasting ZKP models emerged. IBM is building a privacy network on Hyperledger Fabric with no view to third-party visibility — optimised for CBDC use cases, pushing compliance entirely to individual bank perimeters. BIS Project Mandala takes the opposite approach: peer-to-peer ZKP-based compliance pre-validation that integrates with existing compliance systems and allows re-verification of proofs at each hop without re-running checks. The Mandala architecture explicitly names "existing compliance solutions" as integration points — a direct Chainalysis opportunity. The contrast matters: privacy tech can either enable or undermine compliance depending on choices being made right now.

Sources: IBM Showcase (Jordan) BIS Side Event — Project Mandala (Jordan)
→ Inform: Product — Mandala integration is a concrete opportunity (7 central banks). Research — IBM supervisory gap = our positioning. GovTech — DNB contacts obtained.

P1 4. Regulators are actively seeking on-chain analytics capability

AMLA used its first conference to position technology, analytics and AI at the centre of EU AML/CFT supervision — Commissioner Albuquerque signalled AMLA will lead globally including at FATF, and James Lee made the case for blockchain analytics on Panel 3. SEC Crypto Task Force Chair Richard Gabbert expressed strong interest in matching on-chain and off-chain data for market abuse detection, noting assets will be "multi-dimensional" requiring all venues to be monitored. FINMA's innovation team discussed market abuse use cases for CASPs and wants a follow-up Chainalysis presentation. Turkey's TUBITAK is building its own analytics — both opportunity and competitive signal. The Bank of Lithuania's governor publicly agreed with Matthias's points on supervisors using on-chain data for market adoption. IMF is developing stablecoin issuer data standards via their Data Gap Analysis. Standard Chartered's Rene Michau framed the problem perfectly at the PZF TRM workshop:

"Imagine a bank operating blockchain wallets on public chains — a supervisor calls and asks: can you demonstrate you knew who owned that address, the agent had permission, and you didn't disclose PII?" — Rene Michau, Standard Chartered
Sources: AMLA Conference Matthias bilaterals (SEC, FINMA, TUBITAK, IMF) PZF Matthias Panel PZF Workshop — TRM Labs PZF CEO Dialogue
→ Inform: GovTech/Sales — SEC, FINMA, AMLA are live opportunities. Marketing — publish T1/T2 + ontology before AMLA sets benchmarks. Product — TUBITAK building own analytics (competitive signal).

P1 5. Market structure is splitting: retail dead, institutional surging, payments growing

Every exchange at PZF described retail as "effectively dead," yet institutional flows are picking up materially — one Swiss private bank contact reported sharply rising ZCash demand. Stablecoin volumes remain steady despite the crypto downturn, signalling a structural shift toward payments. EUR stablecoins now ~2% of volume (from near zero), with more launching. B2B cross-border is the biggest use case (Asia-Pacific, global south beneficiaries). US banks have more deposits than ever — not a deposit flight story. Correspondent banking for Tier 2/3 banks is very expensive, making stablecoins a genuine alternative.

Sources: PZF Main Stage (Simon Taylor) Matthias bilaterals PZF dinner intel
→ Inform: Risk — ZCash institutional surge (privacy coin implications). Product — EUR stablecoin growth = more issuers. Sales — B2B cross-border APAC is where demand will come.

P1 6. Agentic finance is the next regulatory frontier — and crypto is the rail

MAS is revamping Singapore's payments system specifically for agentic payments. MasterCard is embedding AI risk controls into agentic commerce at network level. Multiple panels agreed crypto rails are the likely infrastructure for agent-to-agent value transfer, though activity today is minimal. UBS expects a high failure rate implementing agents but "some will exceed expectations exponentially." Amazon sees programmability and agentic use as key drivers for stablecoin adoption — "only if rails are regulated." Chris Brummer (Georgetown) noted that fiduciary duties may need to be decomposed into "programmable constituent parts."

Sources: Agentic Finance Panel (Wk 1) TRM Workshop (MasterCard) PZF Main Stage (UBS CEO) Stripe Closed Door (Amazon) Matthias bilaterals (MAS)
→ Inform: Product — agent monitoring is an emerging requirement (MAS building now). Research — position paper before EU AI Act (2 Aug). Marketing — 100M+ x402 is unique and quotable.

P1 7. Tether is repositioning for Europe — and MiCA 2 is the target

Tether has hired local EU policy and gov affairs staff and asked to join BC4EU — causing board-level debate. If MiCA 2 removes obligatory bank deposits from reserve requirements, Tether may re-enter the EU directly and seek to shape the regulation. On stage, Tether CEO claimed "we did more for financial inclusion than all NGOs" and "proved we can redeem 20% overnight — banks can't do that." Behind the scenes, there is industry consensus that USDT has the highest demand (Banking Circle) — raising the question of whether MiCA is working as intended.

  • Project Mandala: Compliance pre-validation for cross-border payments using ZKPs
